Procedures - Tutorial 2

Instruction

Illustration

  1. Open the mapping-result of Exercise1.

Check chapter "How to Operate BIC Mapping Designer" in Training BIC 6 Basics if you need further assistance.
  1. Go to the "Mapping procedures" tab and click on "Convert local procedure to method" button.

Convert local procedure to method

  1. Warning will pop up. A global procedure needs to be created first.

Warning message

  1. Go to Project explorer, rightclick on "Global procedures". Click on "New" > "Global Procedure".

Add a new global procedure

  1. Type in the procedures' name and click OK.

  2. Go back to the "Mapping procedures" tab in the mapping.

Global procedure properties

  1. Click on "Convert local procedure to method" button.

Convert local procedure to method

  1. Choose the global procedure where the local one should be converted to and click OK.

Select global procedure

  1. Have a look at the global procedure now: The local procedure is converted to a global procedure method. It is ready for usage now.

global procedure details

  1. Delete the local procedure by using the "Delete" button in the "Mapping procedures" tab.

delete local procedure

  1. Compile your mapping.
    An error will be displayed.

Error message

  1. Change the procedure call.
 
for LOOP1% = 1 to COUNT_RM1%
    // pick up Output-Parameter of global procedure
    procedure_flag% = ContentCheck_check_role(PARTNERID$[LOOP1%]);
    if procedure_flag% = 1
        copy PARTNERID$[LOOP1%] to ADDRESS.ROLE:NAME;
        copy NAME$[LOOP1%]      to ADDRESS.NAME:NAME;
        copy STREET$[LOOP1%]    to ADDRESS.STREET:STREET;
        copy ZIP$[LOOP1%]       to ADDRESS.ZIP:ZIP;
        copy CITY$[LOOP1%]      to ADDRESS.CITY:CITY;
        copy COUNTRY$[LOOP1%]   to ADDRESS.COUNTRY:COUNTRY;
    endif
next
  1. Check the procedures content.
    Please use only the variables that are really necessary.
 
local role$, result%;

result% = 0;

if role$ = "OSO"
    result% = 1;
endif
if role$ = "AG"
    result% = 1;
endif
if role$ = "WE"
    result% = 1;
endif
if role$ = "SP"
    result% = 1;
endif
if role$ = "OSP"
    result% = 0;
endif

exitproc(result%);

  1. Have a look at the destination-file and see how the "global procedure" worked.
