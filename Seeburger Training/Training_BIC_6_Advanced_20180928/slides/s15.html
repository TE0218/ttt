XML Characteristics - Definition

  • XML = eXtensible Markup Language

“Mark-up language” – Describes data and their structures

“Extensible” – Can be manipulated by the author

Mark-up languages have been designed to add text data AND specific information.
The origin of the markup language is in the letterpress. In addition to the text of the books, they added control information for the printer. They were not printed - they served out to highlight a paragraph, a bold or different type of font.

Since 1985 there is the first markup language SGML (Standard Generalized Language) and the Document Type Definition. However, the 500-page documentation of the SGML language indicates the complexity. Today's markup languages such as HTML and XML derived from SGML.
